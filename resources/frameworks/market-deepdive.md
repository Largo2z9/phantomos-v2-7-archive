# Market Deep-Dive — Analytical Framework

> Reference framework for `study-niche-marketdeepdive`. Names the strategic vocabulary the skill applies selectively when reading a niche. Cited by the SKILL.md, never form-filled into the memo.

The frameworks below are analytical vocabulary, not section templates. The skill picks the 1-3 lenses that are *load-bearing for this niche right now* and ignores the rest. A memo that runs all six layers as a checklist is a doctrine violation: it produces a McKinsey-shaped document instead of an operator-grade strategic read. Layer 6 of the methodology — implications for the operator's brand — is the only mandatory section. Everything else is optional, conditional on signal density.

The unit of analysis is the niche, not the brand. The brand is the lens through which implications get translated in the final synthesis.

## Selective Porter — read industry structure, not five bullets

Porter's Five Forces describes the structural physics of an industry. In the wild, two forces are usually dominant, two are peripheral, one is irrelevant. The skill names the dominant forces and what they imply, never the full enumeration.

The binary tests below decide whether a force is active enough to write about.

| Force | Active when | Analytical question | Source signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rivalry intensity | >30 visible players competing on same wedge OR 3-5 dominant rolling up the long tail | Is the niche fragmenting (room for new positioning) or consolidating (window closing for independents)? | Industry top-N listicles, last 18 months M&A press, retail buyer reports |
| New entrant threat | Average launch frequency in trade press > 1 per quarter on the same sub-vertical | What barrier protects incumbents — formulation, IP, retail shelf, regulatory? Is that barrier eroding? | DTC launch trackers (Modern Retail, Glossy), Shopify-app launch frequency, Crunchbase Series A density |
| Buyer power | Trustpilot/Sephora reviews dominated by price complaints, repeat-purchase commentary about substitutability | Is the category commoditized (price is the conversation) or differentiated (operator can hold premium)? | Review density on price terms, promo cadence on top brands, retail markdowns |
| Supplier power | Single-source actives, IP-locked partnerships, named ingredient supplier on brand technical pages | Can the operator's spec be replicated by competitors, or is there a structural lock-in worth defending? | Brand ingredient sourcing pages, supplier press releases, patent filings |
| Substitute threat | An adjacent category is taking share via search-volume shift or press coverage as the new go-to | Is the category itself at risk of being replaced by a different intervention? | Google Trends crossover, analyst pieces flagging substitution, top-of-funnel ad mix shifts |

The substitute force is the one most often missed and most often load-bearing. GLP-1 took share from weight-loss supplements in 24 months. AI tutoring is taking share from Khan-style apps. Med-spa procedures are taking share from anti-age skincare in the >35 segment. Substitute threat reframes the strategic question: it's not "how do we beat competitors", it's "is the category itself still the right wedge in 24 months".

When two forces are active, the memo writes a paragraph on each. When only one dominates, the memo writes one paragraph and says so. Five-bullet checklists are refused.

## Selective PESTEL — name the macro forces shaping the niche right now

PESTEL is a macro lens. Six forces, rarely more than three active at once. The skill identifies which forces are *currently reshaping the niche*, not which forces theoretically apply.

| Force | Active when | Analytical question | Source signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Political | Recent or pending legislation directly targets the category, or sector subsidies/tariffs are in motion | What policy change closes or opens a window in the next 12-24 months? | Government bulletins, EU Commission consultation pages, trade association lobbying briefs |
| Economic | Category sales correlate with discretionary spending and recession signal is active, or currency exposure is structural | Is demand cyclical, and where are we in the cycle? What does that imply for messaging (value-frame vs aspiration-frame)? | Sector revenue data, consumer confidence indices, FX exposure of input costs |
| Social | A generational cohort or lifestyle shift is reshaping the buyer, with 12-24 months of trend data behind it | Who is the buyer becoming, and how is the offer outdated for that buyer? | Census data, generational consumption studies, lifestyle media coverage density |
| Technological | A new mechanism, ingredient, platform, or automation pattern is moving from lab to consumer in 12-24 months | Does the category get reframed by tech the operator must adopt, or by tech that destroys the category's premise? | VC investment patterns in the sub-vertical, conference keynotes, patent filing trends |
| Environmental | Sustainability mandates, carbon disclosure, or supply-chain ESG pressure is named in retailer requirements or recent legislation | Is the supply chain about to become a competitive axis, and how exposed is the brand? | Retailer compliance memos, EU Green Deal sectoral application, NGO scorecards |
| Legal | Claim restrictions, IP enforcement, or advertising platform policy is tightening on the category | Which claims survive, which become liabilities? What's the backup positioning if the claim is regulated out? | Regulator enforcement actions, advertising platform policy updates, IP litigation press |

For skincare clean FR in 2025, Social (anti-age stigma vs aging-positive) + Technological (peptide synthesis at scale, GLP-1 spillover) + Legal (EU 1223/2009 evolution) are dominant. Economic and Environmental get one line. Political is peripheral. That selection is the analysis. Form-filling all six dilutes signal.

## Schwartz sophistication evolution — read longitudinally, not as a snapshot

Eugene Schwartz's five-stage sophistication ladder describes how a market's messaging tolerance evolves as competitors saturate the obvious angles. Stage 1 — first to make a claim. Stage 2 — claim plus quantifier. Stage 3 — mechanism. Stage 4 — bigger mechanism, often paired with identity. Stage 5 — identity, lifestyle, philosophy; the product is incidental.

The skill reasons longitudinally. Where was the niche three years ago, where is it now, where will it be in three years. That trajectory is what makes the analysis operational, because messaging strategy lags the market: a brand built on stage 3 mechanism reveal in a stage 5 identity-driven market reads as flat-footed and dies slowly.

Skincare clean FR sat at stage 3 in 2020 (mechanism: "what's not in it"), reached stage 4 in 2023 (mechanism + dermo proof: "clean *and* it works"), is at stage 5 in 2026 (identity + lifestyle: "clean is who I am, performance is assumed"). New entrants on a stage-4 angle in 2026 are competing in a market that has moved past their pitch. Implication: the operator either pivots messaging to identity-and-philosophy or finds a contrarian sub-niche where stage 3 still has room.

Nootropics sat at stage 2 in 2018 (single-claim "focus"), at stage 3 in 2022 (named compound + mechanism), at stage 4 in 2025 (stack rationale + bio-data). Stage 5 has not arrived; stack-rationale messaging still wins.

The analytical question is not "what stage is the niche", it is "when does the next stage begin, and is the operator's current positioning ready". Saying *"performance prouvée"* in a niche that just crossed into stage 5 is two years late. Saying it in a niche still consolidating stage 3 is two years early. Timing is the strategic decision.

Awareness distribution feeds this layer mechanically. In a stage-4 niche, the audience is mostly product-aware; in a stage-2 niche, the audience is mostly problem-aware. Messaging strategy follows.

## Crossing the Chasm — only for early-stage niches

Geoffrey Moore's chasm framework applies when the niche is young enough that early-adopter and early-majority buyer behaviors are structurally different. The binary test: is the niche pre-chasm (early adopters dominate, marketing-by-storytelling and visionary positioning works) or post-chasm (early majority dominates, marketing-by-proof, social validation, and category leadership required)?

Apply when: niche is under 5 years old, landscape is fragmented (no clear category leader >25% share), category vocabulary is still being negotiated in trade press. Skip when: niche is mature, category leader is established, sophistication is at stage 4-5. Mature niches have crossed the chasm long ago and the framework adds no signal.

For a pre-chasm niche, the strategic implication is that the operator's growth ceiling is the early-adopter pool, and crossing demands a different muscle (proof artifacts, social validation, retail credibility, named-customer references) than what got them here. For a post-chasm niche, the framework is irrelevant and the memo says so in one line.

GLP-1-adjacent supplements were pre-chasm in 2024, are crossing now. Peri-menopause as a category is pre-chasm. Anti-age skincare crossed in the early 2000s. Naming the chasm is only useful when the niche is actually near it.

## Capital flow and exit comparables — read the M&A and funding landscape

Strategic memos that ignore capital are tactical memos. The capital landscape decides which positionings are durable, which are commoditized, and what window matters for the operator.

Four signal types, captured with sources, surfaced with multiples where disclosed.

Recent deals across the last 18 months: name, acquirer, target, disclosed multiple (revenue × N or EBITDA × N), strategic rationale where stated. Three deals at 4-6× revenue in the same niche signals an active exit window for similarly-sized targets. One deal at 12× revenue with strategic synergy is a one-off, not a market multiple.

Funding rounds: Series A/B/C amounts and disclosed valuations, geography of capital (FR/EU/US), investor pattern (sector specialists vs generalists). Five sector-specialist rounds in 12 months signals the niche is on the institutional radar; generalist-only rounds suggest the niche is still proving itself.

Public market signals: IPOs, secondary acquisitions of public players, comparable trading multiples for category leaders. Public comps anchor private valuations within ~30%.

Strategic acquirer activity: which CPG conglomerates, which platform companies, which roll-up funds are buying in this niche, and on what cadence. L'Oréal acquiring 3 clean-beauty brands in 18 months is a different signal than Unilever doing one opportunistic deal.

The implication for the operator is one of three reads. Window favorable for exit (multiples healthy, strategic acquirers active, comparable size traded recently) — preserve optionality, build clean financials, avoid debt that complicates a deal. Window favorable for raise (institutional capital flowing, dilution acceptable for scale velocity) — sharpen the deck, name the comparables. Window favorable for scale-and-hold (no acquirer pattern, IPO path open at higher revenue threshold) — operate for cash flow, take the long route. The memo names which read applies and why.

Exit comparables are surfaced in the memo only — never written to the core schema. They live in the custom market-memo file as named precedents.

## Disruption signals — read the 12-24 month outlook

Disruption signals are the shifts that can reframe a niche faster than the operator's positioning cycle. Three buckets, each detected by signal density.

Tech disruption arrives from labs and venture rounds 12-24 months ahead of consumer impact. AI in skincare diagnostics. Biotech ingredient platforms. Automation in DTC fulfillment. The detection signal is VC investment pattern, patent filings, conference keynote density. Binary test for activation: is the signal already affecting top-three players' product roadmaps, or is it 12-24 months out? If top players are already adopting, it's no longer a disruption — it's table stakes the operator needs yesterday.

Demographic disruption is slower but harder to escape. Aging Western populations reshape skincare, supplements, healthcare. Gen-Z purchasing power shifts reshape values-driven categories. GLP-1 adoption reshapes nutrition and weight-management adjacencies. Single-person households reshape SKU sizes and meal categories. Detection signal is multi-year cohort data, not press cycles.

Regulatory disruption hits hardest because it's binary. Claim restrictions remove positioning angles overnight (FTC enforcement on health claims, EU 1223/2009 cosmetic claim tightening). Advertising platform policy redefines acquisition economics (Meta political-ads classifications, ad-account suspensions on adjacent categories). Sustainability mandates redefine cost structures. Detection signal is regulator consultation pages, enforcement action density, platform policy update frequency.

For each signal flagged, the memo answers two questions. What is the brand's current exposure? What protects it — IP, supply chain, customer lock-in, brand equity that survives the disruption? A signal without an exposure read is observation, not analysis.

## Strategic implications — synthesis is the only mandatory section

Frameworks become operational here. The synthesis section answers three questions for the operator's brand specifically. It is the only section the memo ships without — the rest are optional based on signal density.

What positioning is durable for 24 months, what is commoditized in 12? Names the angles the operator should defend, the angles the operator should exit, the angles the operator could newly own. Anchored in Schwartz trajectory + selective Porter (substitute threat especially) + capital flow.

What capital window matters — exit, raise, or scale? Names which read applies, sized to the operator's revenue band, with comparable precedents from the capital section. If no window is open, says so plainly: "operate for cash flow, no M&A activity in your size band in 18 months".

What disruption is the brand exposed to, what protects it? Names the two or three disruption signals from the prior section that hit this brand specifically, the exposure path, the structural protection or its absence. Ends with one primary strategic bet, two-to-three secondary bets, recommended cadence to revisit.

This section reads as prose, not as a bullet recap of the framework layers. Frameworks supplied the vocabulary; the synthesis supplies the verdict.

## Anti-patterns

**Framework-as-section-header.** Writing `## Porter's 5 Forces` followed by five bullets is form-fill, not analysis. The frameworks are vocabulary; the memo is prose. If a framework name appears as a heading in the operator-facing memo, the skill failed.

**TAM without source.** A market-size number without a `[MKT-NNN]` corpus pointer is invention. The mutation gate refuses it. Null is a valid answer; fabricated precision is not.

**Single-analyst extrapolation.** One Forbes article does not establish a trend. Triangulation is the rule: a claim ships when two independent sources support it, or one primary-data source does, or one operator-authoritative source does. One opinion piece is a hypothesis, not a finding.

**Twenty-page memo.** The cap is 3000 words, roughly 4-6 pages. If the draft runs longer, the skill rewrites tighter, not ships long. Operators do not read consultant decks. Layer 6 first, decision-first.

**Niche-level analysis bleeding into brand-level.** The skill reads the niche. The brand snapshot lives in `snapshot-brand`, customer voice in `mine-voc`, category voice in `mine-vom`. When the memo starts critiquing the operator's product copy or proposing campaign angles, scope has drifted. The synthesis section translates niche reads into brand implications — it does not rewrite the brand's positioning or propose tactical executions.

**Form-fill on Porter, PESTEL, or Schwartz.** Each framework is selective. Porter — name the 1-2 active forces. PESTEL — name the 2-3 active forces. Schwartz — read the trajectory, not just the current stage. Crossing the Chasm — apply only when the niche is near a chasm. Six layers run in full only when six layers all carry signal, which is approximately never. The discipline is to cut the layers that don't matter, not to fill them out for completeness.
