# Prisms

PhantomOS seen through six angles. None is *the* definition. Each lights a facet of the same product, scoped for a specific reader. Use as canonical snippets in WELCOME, README, skills, pitch, posts, conversations — pick the angle that fits the audience.

Order of prisms is not a ranking. Prism 1 (Economic) and Prism 6 (Product) are the two most common openings — one for macro framing, one for concrete evaluation.

## 1. Economic — The substrate of the agent economy

The agent economy splits the market in two. Enterprise has bespoke encoding systems at inaccessible cost. Everyone else has tools that log without encoding. PhantomOS is for everyone else — operators running their own business, who need the discipline without the budget.

*Audience: investors, strategists, journalists, macro-framed readers.*

## 2. Cognitive — Centralized brand context

Your brand lives in too many places — your head, a Notion doc read once, prompts saved across browser tabs, half-remembered client conversations. PhantomOS gathers it into one local workspace your agent reads on every request. You tell the system something once and it stays. Corrections you make later become rules applied next time, so the context grows denser with use. Day one you stop re-briefing. Month six you have an asset that works alongside you.

*Audience: operators considering the jump.*

## 3. Entrepreneurial — Revenue decoupled from time

PhantomOS breaks the ceiling of billable hours. You encode the system that operates the work — once, then it runs. Revenue stops being a function of your availability.

*Audience: consultants, freelancers, solo operators.*

## 4. Methodological — Context Layering, operationalized

Context engineering got a name in 2025. It never got a runtime. PhantomOS is Context Layering turned into a running workspace: Decision Trace captures the reasoning behind each correction, Skill Graph organizes the atomic nodes an agent navigates, Feedback Loop runs the cycle until the agent stops needing correction.

*Audience: AI engineers, technical founders, readers who want primitives.*

## 5. Strategic — The process moat

The graph is not the moat. An exfiltrated graph is a snapshot. What protects you at six months is the capture discipline — the Decision Traces logged this week, the Feedback Loops that have already run a hundred times. PhantomOS builds the discipline into the workflow, so the graph stays denser than any snapshot-brand of it.

*Audience: operators already convinced the work is encodable, skeptical of durability.*

## 6. Craft articulation — The clarification forced by encoding

PhantomOS makes you say out loud what you usually leave implicit. The positioning you have been pitching from memory becomes a written value proposition. The tone you feel but never describe becomes a tone profile the agent consults before every reply. The objections your offer handles become structured entries in an audience profile. This clarification is not a side-effect. It is half the value. Operators who use PhantomOS for three months report their written method becomes legible to themselves for the first time — which also makes it licensable, transferable, and defendable.

*Audience: consultants, senior operators, and coaches whose expertise was tacit until now.*

## 7. Operational — Parametric composition, coherent output at scale

The agent economy misses the real lever when it thinks in one-shot prompts. The real lever is parametric composition: take a proven craft — creative generation, email flow design, audit scoring — decompose it into atomic variables (mechanics, angles, awareness levels, audience pain points, proof types, tones, formats), store each as a curated bank in the workspace, then let a workflow traverse the matrix of their combinations to generate output at volume. Ask for one hundred Meta statiques: the system does not write a hundred blank prompts, it walks `mechanics × angles × audience × proof × format` and composes each variant grounded in stored atoms. Ask for a Klaviyo flow on a new brand: the system reuses what converted on a sibling brand, crossed with encoded LTV and audience segments. Mass volume does not trade off quality when the atoms are curated and the matrix is respected. This is why PhantomOS ships with registries and routing tables — they are not documentation, they are the parameter space the agent traverses.

*Audience: operators who run scaled operations and recognize that the real bottleneck on AI-generated output is the absence of curated parameter banks, not the absence of a smarter model.*

## 8. Product — What ships today

One workspace per brand, one Agent Contract per workspace. A Context DB of six versioned entities — brand, product, offer, audience, learnings, strategy. Ten operator skills. A write contract gates every mutation. No dashboards, no UI, no real-time sync. Claude Code required.

*Audience: anyone mid-evaluation who wants concrete before abstract.*

## Usage

A prism is a snippet, not a pitch. Drop it verbatim into the surface that needs it. Do not mix prisms in the same paragraph — each one is self-contained and loses force when blended. If a surface needs more than one angle, use them sequentially as distinct blocks, each with its own heading.

When a new surface requires a framing none of the six prisms cover, draft a candidate, run it against the voice canon (`voice.md`), and propose it here. Prisms ship in batches, not one-offs.

> **Why this doc exists:** PhantomOS is a product, a thesis, a method, and a substrate at once. A single definition flattens it. Multiple framings, each with an audience and a mechanism, let the same product address engineers and operators and investors without softening itself to hit all three.
